IBM App

This is a prototype (UI and back-end) my team and I completed within 48 hours for the Cornell's Health Hackathon AI.

The Problem

With the outbreak fo the new coronavirus (COVID-19), many people are uninformed about their personal risk of contracting the disease. This has led to three major challenges:
(1) Information and data are unspecific and only available on a population level. Uninffected individuals find it hard to relate.
(2) The coronavirus can be trnasmitte during the incubation period, meaning every asymptomatic patient may be a career.
(3) Public health authorities can only rely on patient's self-reporting of symptoms which may not always be accurate.

Prototyping and Ideation

Using React-Hook geolocation APIs, we built a mobile app prototype that is able to notify the users if they are at risk of contracting the coronavirus by logging their travel history. Our app collects and stores data of whom the users have come into proximity with in the past 14 days (incubation period). If any of the app user is tested positive for the coronavirus, the app will backtrack all the other users who have come into close contact with the infected user, and send out a notification informing them of their potential exposure.

Technologies Used

The app consists of 4 user interfaces: (1) account-registration, (2) a QR code, (3) a heatmap that shows asymptomatic cases, and (4) a risk status

React-Hook Geolocation APIs

Using the APIs, the server collects the user's travel history and match them with other proximate users

IBM Cloud

The app uses the notification function available on IBM Cloud to send the user's notifications

AI and Big Data

Data is collected and stored in the database to map out the asymptomatic cases in the areas that the users should avoid

QR Code to De-identified Users

To comply with HIPPA, a QR code will be given to the user upon registration as their identification code to ensure anonymity.

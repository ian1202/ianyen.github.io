The Problems

With the outbreak fo the new coronavirus (COVID-19), many people are uninformed about their personal risk of contracting the disease. This is due to:
1. Information is only available on a population level instead of an individual level – we know the number of cases in our city and country only, but not the number of cases specifically in our neighborhood or in places we are planning on going today.
2. The coronavirus can be transmitted during the incubation period- every person can be a career without showing any symptoms and we do not know whether we have personally been in contact with these people.
3. Redundant process of tracking down exposed individuals manually– there is no systematic way to notify the strangers sitting next to you in the bus this morning that you have now tested positive for COVID-19.

Ideation and Prototyping

The App Functionalitiies

Using Esri and Google Maps geolocation APIs, I built a mobile app (web app) that is able to notify the users if they may have been exposed to the coronavirus by logging their geolocation coordinates (longtitude and latitude). The app collects and stores data of whom the users have come into 6-feet proximity with in the past 14 days (incubation period). If any of the app user has tested positive for COVID-19, the app will backtrack all the other users who have come into close contact with this infected user, and send out a notification informing them of their potential exposure.

The App Features


Notification of Status Update

If someone has now tested positive and they have been in contact with the user in the past 14 days, the user will get a notification as well as a change of status (from green to yellow) on their phone. To ensure the integrity of the published data, the doctor of the infected person has to communicate the information outwardly by scanning their QR code.

Anonymity

Every user will be given a randomly generted QR code upon registration with their mobile phone number. In compliance with HIPPA, the app will not collect the user's unique demographic information (name/ address/ insurance, etc) at all. Users will only need to register with their phone number, which will then automatically generate a QR code as their unique identifier.


Heat Map

There is a heat map that maps out all the asymptomatic cases in the user's neighborhood– people who have the "yellow" exposure warning status but have yet officially tested positive for COVID-19

Clinician's Interface

The interface on the right appears on the clinician's phone after they scan the user's QR code. For information integrity, in order for the positively tested patient to push a notification to the exposed users, a clinician is required to scan the user's QR code to verify the information. In consideration of the stigma around COVID-19 and HIPPA regulations, no identifiable demographic information of the positive- patient will be asked in the process. This feature prevents any users from creating false alarms or public panic by faking a positive test result.

Database Design

For this prototype, I have built a Restful API in Node.JS to push the geolocation coordinates of the users to MongoDB (a cloud database). The database consists of all the registered user profiles (as seen on the left hand side below "coroknow_coordinates"). In each of the profiles (User 103 as an example in the figure), there is a log of all the IDs of the users who have been in the 6-feet disrance of that user. The longtitude and latitude coordinates indicate the location at which they came into contact.

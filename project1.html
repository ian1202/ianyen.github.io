"The Coronavirus Tracker" is a prototype (UI and back-end) my team and I completed within 48 hours for
Cornell's Health Hackathon AI.

The Problem

With the outbreak fo the new coronavirus (COVID-19), many people are uninformed about their personal risk of contracting the disease. This has led to three major challenges:
(i) Information and data are unspecific and only available on a population level. Uninffected individuals find it hard to relate.
(ii) The coronavirus can be trnasmitted during the incubation period, meaning every asymptomatic patient may be a career.
(iii) Public health authorities can only rely on patient's self-reporting of symptoms which may not always be accurate.

Prototyping and Ideation

Using React-Hook geolocation APIs, we built a mobile app prototype that is able to notify the users if they are at risk of contracting the coronavirus by logging their travel history. Our app collects and stores data of whom the users have come into proximity with in the past 14 days (incubation period). If any of the app user is tested positive for the coronavirus, the app will backtrack all the other users who have come into close contact with the infected user, and send out a notification informing them of their potential exposure.

The App Features


Anonymity

Every user will be given a randomly generted QR code upon registration with their mobile phone number. The purpose of the QR code will be discussed below.


Heat Map

There is a heat map that maps out all the asymptomatic cases– people who have the "yellow" disease status but have yet been officially tested positive– near the user so that they can be aware.



Notification of Status Update

If someone who has been in contact with the user in the past 14 days is now tested positive, the user will get a notification as well as a change of status (from green to yellow) on their phone. To ensure the integrity of the published data, the doctor of the infected person has to communicate the information outwardly by scanning their QR code.

Challenges Encountered

Because of the extremely tight time limit, we did not have enough time to integrate the backend code with the user interface. Nonetheless, that is something that we have the capacbility and resources to achieve.

Technologies Used

Back-end code available on my Github

React-Hook Geolocation APIs

Using the APIs, the server collects the user's travel history and match them with other proximate users

IBM Cloud

The app uses the notification function available on IBM Cloud to send the user's notifications

AI and Big Data

Data is collected and stored in the database to map out the asymptomatic cases in the areas that the users should avoid

QR Code to De-identified Users

In compliance with HIPPA, the doctor simply have to scan the user's assigned QR code to change their disease status.

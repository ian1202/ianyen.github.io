Google

"Pangea" is a prototype (UI & UX) my team and I completed within 28 hours, sponsored by Google, for
MIT Sloan School of Management's Hack for Inclusion.

The Problem

While travel seems like an ideal way to enable people to understand different cultural perspectives, a vast proportion of tourists never truly step outside their cultural comfort zone to better understand the culture of their destination. People travel to expand their cultural horizons, but they often fail to accomplish this goal by failing to plan a culturally-inclusive itinerary. The most popular attractions are also the first that people find. Designing an itinerary that balances multiple aspects of cultural understanding (such as history, local life, arts, and interaction with locals) is difficult.Therefore, we created a product for the travlers who aim to broaden their cultural horizons, but don't have the local resources necessary to find the ideal experiences to accomplish this goal.

User Research

We first conducted two semi-structured interviews with a product manager from Google Maps and a self-identified transgender person who has experienced discrimination whilte traveling. During and ater each interview, we conducted thorough notes to capture the responses from the interviewees. After that our team gathered together to read and reread all of the data from our own notes. Each of us also contributed to the notes based on our own travel experiences. We then annotated our data with codes by creating a post-it for each of the codes. With the (orange) post-its, we created an affinity diagram by placing one created on the surface at a time, ad rearranging the cards as clusters of similar cards form. Having identified 6 major themes of clusters: "Bias", "Accessibility and Barriers to Travel", "Communication", "Local Perspective", "Overtourism" and "Comfort and Connectedness" we gave each cluster a descriptive label (blue post-its). Finally, we combined the labels and created categories of categories that indicate the most common problems experiened by the travelers.

Design Requirements and Ideation

Of all of the labelled notes, we selected several interesting and representative post-its to deduce an interaction design requirement for each post-it. Following this, we carried out an iterative, fast-moving collaborative process for forming design ideas, in which we switched between idea creation and critiquing. Every team member expressed preliminary design ideas and concepts by creating freehand drawings until we had gathered enough ideas. Then, every team member proceeded to review and judge all of the generated ideas on the excel sheet based on desirable ,viable, and feasible. The items that received the highest overall score were then brought to the table for more spcific idea refinement. We iterated this process until we all believed our ideas had been refined.


App Features

We built an app that helps travelers build an itinerary in a way that pushes them outside their comfort zone while balancing aspects of growth such as history, local life, arts, and interaction.

Personalized Activity Suggestions

It will understand the user by pulling profile information from Google –similar to how Google create relevant advertisements based on your activities and interactions over the internet – and learn their level of comfort for different activities as they accept and reject proposed activities. The red number at the top right of the picture indicates the stretch level of the proposed activity (1 being most comfortable, that is activities that most closely align with what the user normally do in their own country; and 5 being the most stretch, that is activities that are furthest from the user's comfort zone). The app will be biased towards pushing users into their stretch zone.

Report of Stretch Level

Once the user has created a collection of activities for their itinerary, a report indicating will be generated indicating (i) the variety and diversity of the chosen activities (Pie Chart) and (ii) their overall stretch level ("Trip Score").


Learning With and About the Locals

In the next interface, a selection of multimedia learning materials that consist of travel blogs, videos and news articles will be provided so that the user can better understand the culture of their destination -- including cultural norms, language, and expectations. This will help the traveler make the most of their visit as well as ensure smooth interactions with the local population.
